# Listening and Active Communication – Q&A

## Question 1: Steps/Strategies for Active Listening

- Give full attention to the speaker  
- Avoid distractions like mobile or laptop  
- Maintain natural eye contact  
- Do not interrupt  
- Use small verbal cues like “Okay”, “I see”  
- Observe body language and tone  
- Ask clarifying questions  
- Paraphrase what you understood  
- Avoid judging early  
- Respond only after full understanding  

---

## Question 2: Key Points of Reflective Listening (Fisher’s Model)

- Listen carefully to facts and emotions  
- Repeat the message in your own words  
- Acknowledge the speaker’s feelings  
- Confirm understanding: “Did I get it right?”  
- Do not give opinions immediately  
- Show empathy and understanding  

---

## Question 3: Obstacles in Listening Process

- Thinking about what to reply  
- Mobile and other distractions  
- Assuming you already know the topic  
- Emotional reactions (anger, stress, ego)  
- Multitasking  
- Losing focus in long discussions  
- Judging the speaker too early  

---

## Question 4: How to Improve Listening

- Keep phone away  
- Take notes during discussions  
- Practice paraphrasing  
- Pause before replying  
- Ask more questions  
- Observe tone and body language  
- Stay calm during disagreements  

---

## Question 5: When I Use Passive Communication

- Avoid disagreeing with seniors  
- Accept extra workload silently  
- Not asking doubts in meetings  
- Staying silent to avoid conflict  

---

## Question 6: When I Use Aggressive Communication

- During high stress or deadlines  
- When mistakes are repeated  
- In arguments to prove a point  
- When feeling disrespected  

---

## Question 7: When I Use Passive-Aggressive Communication

- Using sarcasm  
- Giving silent treatment  
- Saying “fine” but being upset  
- Gossiping instead of speaking directly  
- Making taunting comments  

---

## Question 8: How to Make Communication Assertive

- Use “I” statements  
- Be clear and direct  
- Keep a calm tone  
- Set boundaries politely  
- Say “No” respectfully  
- Maintain confident body language  
- Respect others’ opinions while expressing yours
