# Good Practices for Software Development

## Question 1: Which point(s) were new to you?

The following points were relatively new or particularly insightful to me:

- The importance of **over-communicating** in a professional environment, especially informing the team proactively about delays or issues.
- The idea that missing calls and not calling back immediately can affect team trust and collaboration.
- Structuring questions properly by clearly explaining the issue and mentioning attempted solutions before asking for help.
- Using tools like **Loom, GitHub Gists, CodeSandbox, and CodePen** to clearly demonstrate problems instead of sending vague messages.
- The concept of **Deep Work** and intentionally blocking distractions such as social media during work hours.
- Managing energy levels through proper food and exercise as part of professional productivity.

These points emphasized that communication and focus are just as important as technical skills in software development.

---

## Question 2: Which area do you think you need to improve on? What are your ideas to make progress in that area?

### Area of Improvement:
I believe I need to improve in **over-communication and structured problem reporting**, especially when I am stuck.

### Plan to Improve:

1. **Better Communication**
   - Inform the team immediately if there are delays or blockers.
   - Use group channels instead of private messages for better visibility.
   - Avoid missing calls and respond promptly.

2. **Structured Problem-Solving Approach**
   - Clearly describe the issue when asking questions.
   - Mention what I have already tried before seeking help.
   - Share screenshots, logs, or code snippets for clarity.

3. **Improve Focus and Productivity**
   - Keep phone on silent during work hours.
   - Block social media apps while working.
   - Practice Deep Work by setting dedicated focus time blocks.
   - Track time to analyze productivity patterns.

4. **Stronger Team Engagement**
   - Join meetings early to build rapport.
   - Understand teammates’ schedules before scheduling discussions.
   - Be mindful of others' time and communicate respectfully.

By consciously applying these improvements, I aim to become more reliable, productive, and effective as a software professional.

---

**Conclusion:**  
Good software development is not only about writing code but also about communication, collaboration, discipline, and consistent self-improvement.
